About Me

I'm currently a Statistics Ph.D. student at UCLA working with Prof. Erin Hartman. I am interested in developing statistical methods to understand causality and complex phenomena. I'm currently working on methods to efficiently generalize experimental results to different populations.

This past summer, I worked at RAND with Lane Burgette, Brian Vegetabile, and the causal tools group to improve covariate balancing methods. In 2019, I spent the summer working at Google with Reid Oda and the quantitative UX research group. I received my B.S. in Mathematics & Economics with a specialization in computing from UCLA in 2017.
